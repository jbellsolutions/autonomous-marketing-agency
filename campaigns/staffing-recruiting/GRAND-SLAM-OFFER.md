The staffing industry is $189 billion. 25,000+ agencies in the US. And it's getting hit from every side:

- AI is replacing the jobs staffing agencies fill
- 81% recruiter burnout rate
- 90% first-year turnover at high-volume agencies
- 60-70% of recruiter time spent on admin, not selling
- 44-day average time-to-fill (getting slower)
- 40% candidate ghosting / no-show rates
- 20-30% annual client churn

61% of agencies claim they "use AI" — but fewer than 10% have it integrated across their full workflow. They bolted on a chatbot or resume parser and called it innovation.

The fear is real: AI is taking their clients' jobs AND their own. The agencies that don't adapt will get consolidated or die.
