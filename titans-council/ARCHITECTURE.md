# Architecture — Titans of Direct Response Mastermind Council

> How 18 legendary copywriter agents, 59 sub-agents, a 3-round council, and a 3-agent Quality Gate turn one offer into production-ready positioning, messaging, strategy, and a complete content operation.

---

## The Core Idea

Napoleon Hill described a practice in *Think and Grow Rich* where he convened an imaginary council of history's greatest minds every night before sleep. He called them his Invisible Counselors. He would present a problem and listen for their counsel.

This system makes that operational. Eighteen AI agents — each one the decoded persuasion architecture of a legendary direct response copywriter or modern business strategist — sit at a virtual Mastermind Table. They independently analyze your offer, then deliberate through three structured rounds, critique each other's work, and produce a consensus deliverable that passes through a 3-agent Quality Gate before anything ships.

The knowledge base powering every agent is the Titan Genome: 19,308 indexed entries drawn from the complete works of 15 direct response legends and 3 modern strategists. Not summaries. Not "inspired by." The actual structural patterns of how they build arguments, write headlines, and move people to action.

## Why Multi-Agent (Not Single Prompt)

A single prompt, no matter how long, produces a single perspective. That is the structural ceiling. Here is why this system uses 18 independent agents instead:

1. **Genuine diversity of frameworks.** Schwartz diagnoses market awareness levels. Hormozi restructures the offer through value equations. Brown builds unique mechanisms. Kennedy writes hard-close sales letters. These are not variations on a theme — they are fundamentally different lenses on the same problem. A single prompt cannot hold 18 contradictory worldviews simultaneously.

2. **Constructive disagreement.** When Schwartz says the market is at sophistication Stage 4 and Brown disagrees, that disagreement is signal. The council rounds force explicit resolution. A single prompt papers over disagreement with compromise.

3. **Credit tracing.** Every idea in every council round is attributed to the agent who originated it. This means the client can trace any headline, hook, or positioning decision back to the specific framework that produced it. A single prompt gives you anonymous output.

4. **Quality through redundancy.** Eighteen independent analyses of the same brief produce a coverage map that no single analysis can match. If only 3 out of 18 agents identify a particular objection, that objection still gets surfaced. A single prompt misses what it misses.

5. **The Quality Gate needs separation.** The Creator, Critic, and Approver must be independent from the council to function as genuine reviewers. You cannot review your own work.

## The Council Model — 3 Rounds

### Why Three Rounds (Not Two, Not Five)

Three rounds map to a proven deliberation pattern: diverge, converge, finalize.

- **Fewer than three** means the council never gets to cross-critique. You get synthesis without pressure-testing.
- **More than three** produces diminishing returns. By Round 3, the disagreements are resolved and the consensus is stable. Additional rounds over-polish without adding substance.

### What Each Round Does

**Round 1 — Synthesis (V1)**
Read all 18 individual takes. Identify recurring themes and standout ideas. Credit every idea to its originator. Note where agents disagree. Produce Version 1 combining the best elements from across the council.

**Round 2 — Cross-Critique (V2)**
Each of the 18 agents critiques V1 through their specific lens. Schwartz checks awareness-level matching. Hormozi pressure-tests the offer stack. Kennedy looks for weak closes. Every change in V2 is documented with who drove it and why.

**Round 3 — Final Consensus**
Final review and production check. The council produces a complete 12-section deliverable:

1. Positioning Statement
2. Category (with dead language / living language table)
3. Unique Mechanism (named, framed, explained)
4. Brand Voice Guide
5. Headline Battery (final 10, production-ready)
6. Hook Arsenal (final 10, with deployment notes)
7. Core Messaging Framework
8. The Offer (restructured, Grand Slam stack)
9. Mechanism Build-Out (E5 framework, funnel architecture)
10. Go-to-Market Playbook
11. Sample Copy Portfolio
12. Upsell Strategy

## The Quality Gate

The Quality Gate is structurally separate from the council. These three agents are independent reviewers, not council members. They have no stake in the output they are evaluating.

### Creator QG
Reviews creative completeness, originality, and production-readiness. Scores 10 dimensions out of 100: positioning clarity, mechanism originality, headline quality, hook strength, voice consistency, offer architecture, mechanism build-out, messaging framework, GTM realism, sample copy quality.

### Critic QG
Adversarial stress-test. Scores 10 dimensions out of 100: claim validity, competitive vulnerability, objection completeness, price-value alignment, proof gaps, channel-message fit, funnel logic, risk reversal strength, scarcity authenticity, implementation reality. Also addresses any flags raised by the Creator.

### Approver QG
Reads both reviews, tallies scores, evaluates all flags. Makes one of three decisions:

| Decision | Criteria | What Happens |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **APPROVED** | Both scores 80+ and zero critical flags | Ships as-is |
| **APPROVED WITH REVISIONS** | Both scores 70+ and flags are addressable | Fixes applied, then ships |
| **SEND BACK** | Either score below 70 or unresolvable flags | Council re-runs Round 3 with QG feedback |

The canonical final output is `round-3-final-approved.md` if the Approver made revisions, or `round-3-final.md` if approved as-is.

## The Titan Genome

The Titan Genome is the knowledge base architecture that makes every agent more than a persona. It is 19,308 indexed entries across 650,000+ words of source material: transcripts, speeches, emails, sales letters, books, and frameworks from 18 legends.

### Architecture

```
Titan-Genome-Content-Multiplier/
├── SWIPE_FILE_CONTEXT.md         <- The Genome: decoded patterns per legend
├── CLIENT_CONFIG.md              <- Client-specific configuration template
├── templates/                    <- 13 content project templates
│   └── PROJECT_{N}_*.md
├── docs/                         <- Process documentation, guides
│   ├── PROCESS_DOCUMENTATION.md
│   └── TEMPLATE_GUIDE.md
└── swipe-file/                   <- Original council agent definitions
    └── council/
        ├── COUNCIL.md            <- Council architecture and roster
        └── agents/*.json         <- Agent config (triggers, style, sources)
```

### How It Works

The Genome is **pattern-based, not copy-based.** Agents do not regurgitate Schwartz's headlines or Kennedy's sales letters. They apply the structural patterns those legends used:

- Schwartz's 5 levels of market awareness as a diagnostic framework
- Hormozi's value equation (Dream Outcome / Time x Effort x Risk) as an offer restructuring tool
- Brown's E5 method and unique mechanism architecture
- Abraham's Strategy of Preeminence positioning model
- Bencivenga's proof-stacking and control-beating patterns

Each agent's definition file (`agents/*.md`) contains: a system prompt encoding the legend's voice and philosophy, a sub-agent roster (3-5 specializations), style traits, activation triggers, and a prescribed approach to new projects. The SWIPE_FILE_CONTEXT serves as shared reference DNA that all agents draw from.

## Agent Design Pattern

Every agent follows a consistent structure:

```
agents/{agent_key}.md
├── Identity          — Era, role, one-line positioning
├── System Prompt     — Full voice: philosophy, methodology, beliefs
├── Sub-Agents        — 3-5 specializations (e.g., schwartz.headlines)
├── Style Traits      — How the agent writes and thinks
├── Best For          — When to call this agent to the table
├── Activation Triggers — Keywords that invoke this agent via the router
└── Approach          — How this agent diagnoses a new brief
```

**Sub-agents** handle distinct facets of a legend's expertise. Schwartz has 5 (awareness, sophistication, headlines, research, brilliance). Hormozi has 3 (offers, value, scaling). The sub-agent count reflects the breadth of the original legend's documented work.

**Leadership agents** (Powell, Marquet, Sinek) follow the same pattern but live in `agents/leadership/` and are invoke-only. They do not run in the default pipeline.

**Quality Gate agents** live in `agents/quality_gate/` and follow a different pattern: they have scoring rubrics instead of style traits, and they run automatically after the council, not during it.

## What Is Intentionally Not Here

- **No code generation.** This system produces content, positioning, messaging, and strategy. It does not generate software.
- **No API layer.** The pipeline runs inside Claude Code as a conversation. There is no REST API, no webhook, no external service integration.
- **No automation beyond content.** The system does not post to social media, send emails, or deploy landing pages. It produces the content; the client deploys it.
- **No persistent agent memory.** Each project is isolated. Agents do not carry knowledge from one project to another. This is intentional — it prevents cross-contamination between clients.
- **No real-time data.** Agents work from the Titan Genome (static knowledge base) and the project brief. They do not pull live market data, competitor pricing, or social analytics.

## Error Philosophy

### When the Council Disagrees

Disagreement is signal, not failure. Round 1 captures disagreements explicitly. Round 2 forces resolution through cross-critique. If two agents hold opposing positions and neither yields, both positions are documented in the final deliverable with reasoning. The client decides.

### When the Quality Gate Rejects

The Approver has three options: approve, revise, or send back. A "send back" means the council re-runs Round 3 with the specific QG feedback attached. This is not a failure — it is the system working as designed. The QG exists precisely to catch what the council missed.

### When an Agent Produces Slop

Rule 51 (Anti-Slop Writing Protocol) and Rule 43 (Anti-Hallucination Protocol) from the operational rules apply. If output reads as generic AI text rather than carrying the distinct voice of the agent's legend, the Critic QG will flag it. The scoring rubric penalizes voice inconsistency, generic claims, and missing proof structures. The structural defense against slop is redundancy: 18 agents producing independent takes means no single agent's weak output can dominate the final deliverable.

### When the Brief Is Insufficient

The pipeline does not guess. Step 1 of CLAUDE.md requires follow-up questions until the brief contains: product/service/offer, target audience, differentiator, desired outcome, deliverable type, and existing assets. If the user provides a one-liner, Claude asks for more before any agents run.
