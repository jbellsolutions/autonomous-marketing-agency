## I. Unanimous Consensus

These are the points where ALL or nearly all 18 legendary copywriters arrived at the same conclusion independently. When 18 minds working in isolation converge on the same answer, that is the strongest signal possible.

### 1. Kill the "AI Agency" Label Immediately (18/18)

Every single copywriter -- without exception -- said the same thing: Callum must never call himself an AI agency again. The phrase is poison. It commoditises him instantly, groups him with thousands of undifferentiated operators, and triggers the scepticism that prospects have built up after being pitched by dozens of identical providers.

- **Schwartz:** "Kill the phrase 'AI agency' immediately. That phrase is dead."
- **Kennedy:** "Every time Callum introduces himself as an AI agency, automation company, or anything adjacent, he gets filed in the same mental drawer as a thousand other operators."
- **Hormozi:** "AI agency language -- dead. Buried. Never say it again."
- **Perry Marshall:** "The moment he says 'AI agency,' he is competing with ten thousand other people."
- **Liam Ottley:** "The moment he says 'AI agency,' he is in a category with ten thousand other providers."

**Council Verdict:** This is non-negotiable. The label dies today.

### 2. Lead with Revenue/Outcome Language, Not Technology (18/18)

Every copywriter independently concluded that AI should be buried deep in the copy as the mechanism, never in the headline or lead. The prospect does not care about AI. They care about leads being worked, revenue being recovered, and deals being closed.

- **Schwartz:** "The word 'AI' should appear deep in the copy, as the explanation of how the mechanism works. Not in the headline."
- **Jay Abraham:** "Always lead with the business owner's problem, never with the technology."
- **Sugarman:** "Never lead with AI. Always lead with the problem or the outcome."
- **Bencivenga:** "I don't sell AI. I stop revenue leaks."

**Council Verdict:** AI is the engine under the bonnet. Revenue is the headline. Always.

### 3. The "Install and Run" Model Is the True Differentiator (18/18)

Every copywriter identified the same competitive advantage: Callum does not build and leave. He installs and stays. This is the structural moat that separates On Auto from every other player in the market.

- **Abraham:** "You are the only person in this market who installs revenue infrastructure and then stays to run it."
- **Hormozi:** "Everyone else in this space does one of three things: sells software, sells consulting, or sells a one-off build. Callum does a fourth thing."
- **Kurtz:** "This is where the relationship deepens and the lifetime value compounds."
- **Lead Gen Jay:** "Most AI agencies sell the build. Callum sells the run."

**Council Verdict:** "Installed and operated" is the core positioning truth. It must appear in every piece of communication.

### 4. The Revenue Leak Audit Is the Entry Point (17/18)

Nearly every copywriter recommended the same front-end offer: a free Revenue Leak Audit that maps the prospect's sales process and quantifies where money is disappearing. This positions Callum as a diagnostician rather than a salesman and creates a natural bridge to the paid engagement.

- **Todd Brown:** "The audit IS the sales process."
- **Kennedy:** "Lead with the audit, not the product."
- **Abraham:** "The most powerful first step is a free revenue leak audit."
- **Bencivenga:** "The Revenue Leak Audit is the perfect entry point."

**Council Verdict:** The Revenue Leak Audit is the top-of-funnel offer. Everything drives toward it.

### 5. Reframe from "Sales Problem" to "Systems/Infrastructure Problem" (18/18)

Every copywriter reframed the core message identically: the prospect does not have a sales problem, a people problem, or a marketing problem. They have a systems problem. A process problem. An infrastructure problem. This reframe is the intellectual foundation of all messaging.

### 6. The Dead Lead Database Is the Trojan Horse (16/18)

The overwhelming majority identified the reactivation angle as the most irresistible entry point. Every business has a CRM graveyard of leads they already paid for. Offering to recover revenue from that database is low-risk, high-impact, and requires no new marketing spend.

- **Abraham:** "The Reactivation Agent is the Trojan Horse. Lead with this in every conversation."
- **Hormozi:** Positioned the Dead Lead Revival Campaign as Bonus 1 in the Grand Slam Offer.
- **Bill Mueller:** Built his entire sample copy around the dead leads narrative.

### 7. Document Results from Two Clients Obsessively (17/18)

Nearly every copywriter emphasised the same urgent action: get detailed metrics from the two existing clients immediately. Specific numbers -- response time before/after, leads recovered, revenue attributed -- are worth more than any headline.

### 8. AIOS Should Be Held Back as a Premium Upsell (18/18)

Universal agreement: do not lead with the AIOS concept in cold outreach. It is too abstract for first contact. AIOS is the back-end ascension offer for clients already running the core system.

### 9. The UK Market Positioning Is an Advantage (12/18)

Multiple copywriters noted that the UK market is actually less saturated than the US for this specific positioning, and that British business owners respond better to understated competence than American-style hype.

## III. Standout Ideas

These are brilliant ideas from individual copywriters that others missed or underemphasised.

### 1. The Enquiry Form Test (Jon Buchan)
Actually fill out the prospect's enquiry form at 11pm, time how long it takes to get a response, and use the real data in the outreach email. This is concrete, personalised, impossible to argue with, and demonstrates the problem in real time. Buchan's cold email using this technique is one of the strongest pieces of outreach copy across all 18 takes.

### 2. The Grand Slam Offer Stack (Alex Hormozi)
Hormozi was the only copywriter to restructure the entire offer using value stacking. His breakdown -- perceiving the total value at GBP 17,500+ against an actual price of GBP 3,000 setup + GBP 500/month -- creates a 5:1 value-to-price ratio. His bonuses (Dead Lead Revival Campaign, 60-Second Response Guarantee, Revenue Leak Report, Sales Team Handoff Playbook) are immediately implementable.

### 3. The Pay-for-Performance Guarantee (Alex Hormozi)
Hormozi proposed a GBP 10,000 revenue guarantee in 90 days or Callum works for free. No other copywriter went this far on risk reversal. This is aggressive but potentially the fastest way to close deals with limited social proof.

### 4. The "Midnight Test" Content Series (Joe Sugarman)
A recurring content series challenging business owners to enquire with their own company after hours and see what happens. This is a zero-cost, curiosity-driven content hook that creates demand organically.

### 5. The Historical Technology Parallel (Ken McCarthy)
McCarthy uniquely drew the parallel between 2026 AI and 1997 websites: "In 1997, every business needed a website and most did not have one. In 2026, every business needs revenue infrastructure and most do not have it." This elevates the positioning from optional upgrade to historical inevitability.

### 6. The Live Demonstration Model (Greg Renker)
Renker was the only copywriter to build a complete 18-minute VSL script around a live demonstration -- submitting a lead on camera and showing the system responding in real time. This is the most powerful proof format identified by any member of the council.

### 7. The Identity Shift Framework (Tom Bilyeu)
Bilyeu uniquely framed the messaging as an identity transformation: FROM "a business that chases leads" TO "a business that never lets a single opportunity die -- a business that runs on auto." This tribal positioning ("businesses that run on auto") creates a community and movement rather than just a customer base.

### 8. The 80/20 Market Layering (Perry Marshall)
Marshall's four-layer market breakdown (80% commodity, 16% professional, 3.2% infrastructure, 0.64% intelligence) precisely identified where Callum sits and why that position is defensible. This framework is useful for internal strategic planning.

### 9. The Broadcast/Audio Channel Strategy (Fred Catona)
Catona was the only copywriter to identify podcast advertising and audio content as a primary channel for UK SMB owners. His 60-second radio spot script is ready to record.

### 10. The Joint Venture Engine (Jay Abraham)
Abraham uniquely recommended building a referral partnership system with complementary service providers (marketing agencies, CRM consultants, business coaches) who are already inside the businesses Callum wants to reach. This is the highest-leverage growth strategy for the early stage.

### 11. The Monthly Revenue Report as Retention Tool (Jay Abraham, Gordon Grossman)
Both recommended a monthly report delivered to each client showing exactly what the system did -- leads captured, follow-ups executed, reactivations triggered, revenue attributed. This makes value visible and makes cancellation psychologically difficult.

### 12. The List-First Outbound Strategy (Gordon Grossman)
Grossman uniquely recommended starting with warm outbound to Callum's existing network (Phase 1), then building a referral engine (Phase 2), and only scaling to cold outbound (Phase 3) once case studies exist. This is the most realistic go-to-market sequence for someone with two clients.

## IV. Points of Disagreement

### Disagreement 1: Mechanism Name

The council produced multiple competing names for the unique mechanism:

| Name | Author(s) | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| The Installed Sales Floor | Schwartz | Visual, physical, picture-word rich | May feel too metaphorical for analytical buyers |
| Installed Revenue Infrastructure | Jay Abraham | Professional, category-creating | Slightly abstract |
| The Revenue Leak Protocol | Todd Brown | Diagnostic, proprietary-sounding, action-oriented | "Protocol" may feel clinical |
| The Revenue Leak Audit and Permanent Seal System | Kennedy, Bencivenga | Descriptive, clear | Too long for a name |
| Revenue Infrastructure as a Service (RIaaS) | Hormozi | Tech-savvy, category-creating | Too jargon-heavy for SMB owners |
| The Revenue Gap | Sugarman | Simple, visual | Less proprietary-feeling |
| The 60-Second Safety Net | Bill Mueller | Specific, vivid | Only captures one of three benefits |
| The Revenue Plug | Jon Buchan | Simple, visual | Slightly informal |
| The Revenue Recovery Engine | Fred Catona, Gordon Grossman | Active, mechanical, broadcast-friendly | Less diagnostic than "protocol" |
| The Revenue Retrieval Engine | Greg Renker | Active, proprietary, demonstrable | Similar to Recovery Engine |

**Council Resolution (Version 1):** The winning mechanism name is **"The Revenue Leak Protocol"** (Todd Brown) for the following reasons:
1. It frames the approach as diagnostic first (finding leaks), then prescriptive (sealing them)
2. "Protocol" implies a systematic, repeatable, professional-grade process
3. It is short enough to use in headlines and conversation
4. It immediately separates from "AI agents" or "automation" language
5. It creates a natural three-phase narrative (Audit, Seal, Lock) that maps perfectly to how the service actually works

The **"Installed Sales Floor"** (Schwartz) is the strongest alternative for specific contexts -- particularly landing pages and email copy where picture-word language has more room to breathe.

The **"Revenue Recovery Engine"** (Catona/Grossman) is the best choice for broadcast and audio contexts where mechanical simplicity matters.

### Disagreement 2: How Bold to Go on Guarantees

- **Conservative camp** (Schwartz, Bencivenga, Kennedy, Kurtz): No explicit guarantee. Let the audit and proof do the work. Too early for guarantees with only two clients.
- **Aggressive camp** (Hormozi): GBP 10,000 revenue guarantee in 90 days or work for free. The guarantee does the selling when proof is thin.
- **Middle camp** (Todd Brown): No hard guarantee but a soft risk-reversal: "If we do not identify leaks worth at least 10x the setup cost, we will tell you."

**Council Resolution (Version 1):** Start with Todd Brown's soft risk-reversal approach. Graduate to Hormozi's performance guarantee once three to five clients have been delivered and documented. The guarantee becomes more powerful as proof accumulates to back it up.

### Disagreement 3: Pricing Direction

- **Hold pricing** (Schwartz, Bencivenga, Lead Gen Jay): Current pricing is fine for early traction. Do not change it.
- **Raise pricing quickly** (Hormozi, Kennedy, Abraham, Perry Marshall): The pricing is already too low for the value delivered. By client five, it should be GBP 5,000+ setup and GBP 1,000+ monthly.
- **Value-based pricing** (Todd Brown, Hormozi): Pricing should scale with the size of the identified revenue leak.

**Council Resolution (Version 1):** Keep current pricing for clients three through five while building case studies. Introduce a higher tier at client six based on documented results. Move toward value-based pricing once the audit process can reliably quantify the revenue leak figure.

### Disagreement 4: Primary Channel

- **LinkedIn organic** (Bencivenga, Liam Ottley, Kennedy): The ICP is on LinkedIn. Content plus DM outreach.
- **Cold email** (Lead Gen Jay): Build a cold email system targeting UK SMB founders.
- **Podcast/audio** (Catona): UK SMBs consume audio content. Less competition.
- **Video/demonstration** (Greg Renker): The product is demonstrable. Video is the primary proof format.
- **Partnerships/JVs** (Abraham, Kurtz): Partner with marketing agencies, CRM consultants, and business coaches.

**Council Resolution (Version 1):** LinkedIn organic as the primary channel (lowest cost, highest relevance for UK B2B SMB). Cold email as secondary. Partnerships as the highest-leverage growth play to build in parallel. Video demonstrations for proof content. Podcast sponsorship once revenue supports it.

### Unique Mechanism (Named and Framed)

**Winner: The Revenue Leak Protocol**

Every business with a sales pipeline has revenue leaks -- invisible points where money falls through and disappears. These leaks exist in three places:

**Leak 1: The Speed Gap** -- Leads come in after hours or during busy periods and sit untouched for hours. By the time someone responds, the prospect has already spoken to a competitor. (Source: Identified by all 18 copywriters)

**Leak 2: The Follow-Up Failure** -- Prospects no-show, proposals go unanswered, warm leads go cold -- and nobody follows up consistently. Most sales require five or more touches. Most teams give up after one or two. (Source: Perry Marshall, Todd Brown, Jon Buchan all cited the "80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups, 44% of reps give up after one" data point)

**Leak 3: The Dead Database** -- Every business has a CRM graveyard of leads they already paid to acquire. Nobody has touched them in months. That is not a dead database. That is buried revenue. (Source: Universal across all 18 takes)

**The Revenue Leak Protocol is a three-phase system:**

1. **Phase 1: The Audit** -- Map the sales process end to end. Identify every leak. Quantify the cost in pounds per month. (Source: Todd Brown's E5 framework)
2. **Phase 2: The Seal** -- Install purpose-built AI agents at each leak point: a Speed-to-Lead Agent (60-second response, 24/7), a Follow-Up Agent (multi-touch CRM-triggered sequences), and a Reactivation Agent (personalised dead-lead outreach). (Source: Original brief + mechanism elaboration from all copywriters)
3. **Phase 3: The Lock** -- Manage and optimise the systems on a monthly retainer. The leaks stay sealed permanently. The system gets smarter over time. (Source: Universal -- the "installed and operated" differentiator)

**Why "Revenue Leak Protocol" won:**
- Todd Brown designed it, and the client specifically requested a Todd Brown build-out
- 16 of 18 copywriters independently used "revenue leak" language
- "Protocol" implies systematic, professional, repeatable process
- It reframes the conversation from "Do I need AI?" to "Am I losing money?" -- which is always yes
- It creates a natural diagnostic entry point (the audit) before any sale

### Branding Direction

**Voice:** Quiet authority. Direct. No hype. Callum should sound like a skilled tradesman who has seen the inside of a hundred sales operations and knows exactly where the rot is. Not a tech bro. Not a consultant. Not a guru. An operator who installs and runs systems.

**Tone attributes (synthesised from all 18):**
- **Direct** -- Short sentences. Plain language. No buzzwords.
- **Diagnostic** -- Default mode is "assessing your situation," not "selling you something."
- **Grounded** -- Everything tied to real money. Real timelines. Real numbers.
- **Physical** -- Use language that creates pictures: install, build, wire, plug, seal, run.
- **Certain** -- Not "we might help." Instead: "This is what we install. This is what it does."
- **British** -- Understated competence. No American-style hype. Straight-talking.

**Personality:** The Infrastructure Man. The person you call when you realise your sales process is held together with tape and good intentions and you want it rebuilt properly. (Source: Schwartz, with reinforcement from Buchan, Mueller, Kennedy)

**Words to use:** Install. Run. Seal. Plug. Recover. Respond. Capture. Pipeline. Infrastructure. Revenue. System. Audit. Build. Operate. Manage.

**Words to avoid:** Revolutionise. Transform. Leverage. Seamless. Cutting-edge. Game-changing. Ecosystem. Solution (as standalone noun). AI-powered (as leading descriptor). Chatbot. Synergy. Disrupt. Deploy.

**Brand tagline candidates:**
1. "Revenue infrastructure, installed and running." (Perry Marshall / Liam Ottley)
2. "We seal the leaks in your sales pipeline. Permanently." (Kennedy)
3. "Your pipeline never sleeps." (Catona)
4. "I don't sell AI. I stop revenue leaks." (Bencivenga)

**Version 1 recommendation:** "Revenue infrastructure, installed and running." -- It is clean, professional, category-creating, and works across all channels.

### Unified Messaging Framework

#### Core Message
Your business does not have a sales problem. It has an infrastructure problem. You have leads. You have talent. But between marketing generating interest and sales closing deals, there is a gap -- and revenue is leaking through it every single day. On Auto finds those leaks, seals them with installed AI infrastructure, and runs the systems permanently so the leaks never reopen.

#### Three Pillars of Proof

**Pillar 1: Speed Captures Revenue**
Every lead responded to within 60 seconds, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Not routed to a voicemail. Not queued for the morning. Responded to, qualified, and booked. Research shows a 391% improvement in conversion when response time drops below one minute. Your competitors are sleeping. Your infrastructure is not.

**Pillar 2: Persistence Closes Deals**
Every prospect followed up with the right message at the right time, triggered by CRM actions. 80% of sales require five or more touches. 44% of salespeople give up after one. On Auto never gives up, never gets busy, and never forgets. Every no-show gets re-engaged. Every stalled deal gets nudged. Nothing falls through.

**Pillar 3: Recovery Monetises Sunk Cost**
Every cold lead in the database gets re-engaged with personalised outreach. These are leads you already paid for. Money you already spent. Sitting in your CRM, untouched, depreciating to zero. The Reactivation Agent turns that dormant database into live pipeline -- recovering revenue from marketing spend that was generating zero return.

#### Key Proof Points
- 60-second lead response time (vs. industry average of multiple hours)
- 24/7/365 operation with zero staffing requirements
- Installed directly into existing CRM -- no new software to learn
- Managed and optimised monthly -- not a one-time project handoff
- Cost comparison: GBP 500/month vs. one additional SDR at GBP 30,000-40,000/year
- Two businesses already running on the system (document specific results immediately)

### Todd Brown Build-Out Summary

Since the client specifically requested a full Todd Brown build-out, here is the complete summary of Todd Brown's contribution.

#### The E5 Framework for On Auto

**1. ENGAGE -- Capture Attention**

Primary engagement mechanism: The Revenue Leak Calculator -- a simple interactive tool or assessment that lets a business owner answer 5-7 questions about their sales process and receive an estimated monthly revenue leak figure.

Engagement messages:
- "Your business is losing between 15-40% of potential revenue to invisible process gaps."
- "You do not have a lead generation problem. You have a revenue leak problem."

Format: Short-form video content (60-90 seconds) demonstrating a single revenue leak scenario.

**2. EDUCATE -- Teach the Mechanism**

Primary education vehicle: A 45-60 minute webinar called "The Revenue Leak Workshop."

Workshop structure:
1. The Invisible Problem (10 min) -- Show that revenue leaks exist in every business
2. The Three Leak Points (15 min) -- First Contact Lag, Follow-Up Failure, Pipeline Abandonment
3. Why Traditional Fixes Fail (10 min) -- Why hiring and ads do not fix structural leaks
4. The Revenue Leak Protocol reveal (15 min) -- Three-phase system with before-and-after proof
5. The Path Forward (5 min) -- Invite qualified businesses to apply for a Revenue Leak Audit

Supporting content: Weekly email sequence (3 emails per week for 2 weeks), LinkedIn "Revenue Leak of the Week" series.

**3. EXCITE -- Build Desire**

Three excitement drivers:
- **The Math Story:** Show the prospect that sealing leaks turns 12 deals into 20 with the same ad spend
- **The Time Story:** "Imagine waking up and finding 14 leads were responded to overnight, 3 appointments booked, 2 cold leads reactivated"
- **The Competitive Advantage Story:** "Your competitors are still responding the next morning. You are operating in a different league."

**4. ENLIST -- Get Commitment**

Primary enlistment vehicle: The Revenue Leak Audit Application (not a "book a call" button).

Application form (10-12 questions) that:
- Qualifies the prospect (revenue range, team size, lead volume)
- Gets them to self-diagnose their leaks
- Creates investment of time and thought
- Positions Callum as selective

Enlistment amplifiers:
- Scarcity: "We install for a maximum of 4 new clients per month"
- Risk reversal: "If we cannot find leaks worth at least 10x our setup cost, we will tell you"

**5. EXECUTE -- Close**

The close happens on the audit call itself:
1. Discovery (10 min) -- Walk through the current sales process
2. Diagnosis (10 min) -- Identify leaks, estimate revenue impact
3. Prescription (10 min) -- Present the Revenue Leak Protocol
4. Decision (5 min) -- Present investment, framed against the leak figure

#### Mechanism Reveal Strategy

The Revenue Leak Protocol is revealed in stages, not all at once:

- **Cold traffic** sees only the problem frame: "You have revenue leaks you cannot see"
- **Warm traffic** (opted in) sees the mechanism name and three phases
- **Engaged traffic** sees the full mechanism with proof and case studies
- **Audit call** gets the personalised mechanism applied to their specific business

This staged revelation builds belief progressively rather than overwhelming with information upfront.

#### Funnel Architecture

Todd Brown designed a complete 7-stage funnel:

1. **Cold Traffic / First Touch** -- LinkedIn content, ads, short-form video. Message: "You don't have a lead problem. You have a revenue leak problem." CTA: Revenue Leak Assessment / Workshop.
2. **Warm Traffic / Opted In** -- Email sequence, workshop replay, retargeting. Message: "Three structural leaks in every pipeline." CTA: Apply for audit.
3. **Engaged / Considering** -- Case study emails, FAQ content. CTA: Complete application.
4. **Applied / Pre-Audit** -- Confirmation emails pre-framing the audit call.
5. **Audit Call / Close** -- Live diagnostic session leading to prescription.
6. **Post-Close / Onboarding** -- Welcome sequence, onboarding checklist.
7. **Retention / Ascension** -- Monthly reports, quarterly reviews, AIOS introduction.

Key architectural decisions:
- Application model (not open cart) -- prospects apply, Callum selects
- The audit is the sales process -- it demonstrates value before money changes hands
- Limited capacity (4 installs per month) -- real scarcity drives urgency
- AIOS is Stage 7 only -- never sold cold, always earned through results
