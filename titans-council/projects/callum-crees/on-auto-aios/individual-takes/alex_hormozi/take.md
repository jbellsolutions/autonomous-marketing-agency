## Positioning Analysis

Here is the problem with this offer right now: Callum is selling a commodity in a commodity wrapper.

Every kid with a Zapier account and a ChatGPT API key is calling themselves an "AI automation agency." The market is Stage 5 sophistication — prospects have heard every claim, seen every demo, been burned by at least one "AI guy." Callum is swimming in a red ocean and doesn't even know it.

But here is what Callum actually has that nobody else does: **he doesn't hand you a system and walk away. He installs it AND runs it.** That is a fundamentally different value proposition. That is not a project. That is infrastructure. That is the difference between buying a treadmill and hiring a personal trainer who shows up every morning.

The positioning shift is simple:

**Stop selling AI automation. Start selling revenue infrastructure.**

Callum is not an AI agency. He is a revenue infrastructure partner. He installs the pipes. He turns on the water. He monitors the pressure. And the client just watches the pipeline fill.

Through the value equation lens:

- **Dream Outcome:** A sales pipeline that runs itself. Leads worked in 60 seconds. Follow-ups that never miss. Dead leads brought back to life. Revenue recovered while you sleep.
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement:** HIGH — because Callum runs it for you. You are not buying software you have to figure out. You are buying an outcome.
- **Time Delay:** LOW — Audit, build, run. Systems go live fast. Not a 6-month consulting engagement.
- **Effort & Sacrifice:** NEAR ZERO — This is the killer. The client does almost nothing. No learning curve. No new software to master. No hiring. No training. Callum's team does it all.

When you decrease effort and time to near zero while keeping dream outcome and likelihood high, you get an offer people feel stupid saying no to.

## Unique Mechanism

The unique mechanism is what I call **The Installed Infrastructure Model.**

Everyone else in this space does one of three things:

1. **Sells software** — "Here's our AI chatbot platform, good luck." Client has to set it up, maintain it, figure it out.
2. **Sells consulting** — "We'll advise you on your AI strategy." Client gets a PDF and a handshake.
3. **Sells a one-off build** — "We'll build your automation." Client gets a Frankenstein system that breaks in 3 months when nobody maintains it.

Callum does none of these. He does a fourth thing that doesn't exist in the market yet:

**He installs and operates revenue infrastructure inside your business on an ongoing basis.**

This is not "done for you" in the traditional sense. This is "installed and run for you." The mechanism is: your sales infrastructure becomes a managed utility. Like electricity. You don't generate your own power. You plug in and it works. Someone else maintains the grid.

The mechanism name: **Revenue Infrastructure as a Service (RIaaS).**

Or simpler for market-facing language: **The On Auto Method** — a three-phase installation of AI-powered revenue infrastructure that turns your sales pipeline into a managed utility.

The three phases become the mechanism story:

1. **The Revenue Leak Audit** — We find where money is falling through the cracks
2. **The Infrastructure Install** — We build and plug in the agents specific to your business
3. **The Managed Run** — We operate and optimise the system monthly so it compounds

This is not automation. This is not AI consulting. This is installed, managed revenue infrastructure. That is the mechanism. That is the thing nobody else is selling.

The current pricing (£500 deposit, £2,500 on completion, £500/month) is fine for early stage but needs reframing:

- Kill the word "deposit." Call it the "Audit Fee" — £500 to run the Revenue Leak Audit. If they move forward, it gets credited toward the install.
- The £2,500 becomes the "Infrastructure Install Fee"
- The £500/month becomes the "Managed Operations Fee"
- Total first-year value to client: measurably more revenue. Total first-year cost: £8,500. If the system generates even one extra deal per month, it pays for itself many times over.

For AIOS, price it at £10,000+ setup with £2,000+/month. This is a premium product for businesses doing £2M+ revenue. Custom scope, custom pricing, but always anchored to the ROI the system generates.

### The Guarantee

This is where most people get scared. Here is what I would offer:

**The "Pay For Performance" Guarantee:** If the system does not generate at least £10,000 in recovered or new revenue in the first 90 days, Callum works for free until it does.

This is aggressive. It is also the single fastest way to close deals when you have no brand recognition and only two case studies. The guarantee does the selling for you.

## Branding Direction

**Voice:** Direct. Technical enough to be credible. Simple enough to be understood by a business owner who doesn't care about AI — they care about revenue. No jargon. No buzzwords. No "leveraging AI to synergise your pipeline." Kill that language.

**Tone:** Confident operator. Not a salesperson. Not a guru. Callum should sound like the guy who quietly runs the infrastructure behind businesses that are growing fast. Think: the engineer who built the machine, not the marketer who sells the dream.

**Personality Archetype:** The Installer. The guy who shows up, opens the hood, finds the leaks, fixes them, and hands you the keys with the engine running. Practical. Competent. No-nonsense.

**What to avoid:**
- "AI agency" language — dead. Buried. Never say it again.
- Hype language — "revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge." These words mean nothing.
- Tech-first language — Prospects don't care about agents, LLMs, or automation flows. They care about leads, revenue, and not losing deals.

**What to lean into:**
- Infrastructure language — "install," "build," "run," "operate," "manage"
- Revenue language — "recover," "capture," "convert," "close"
- Certainty language — "guaranteed," "every lead," "24/7," "zero missed"

**Brand name:** "On Auto" works. It is simple, memorable, and descriptive. Keep it.

## Strategic Notes

### What I Would Do Differently

**1. Kill the "AI agency" label immediately.**
Callum needs to position himself as a revenue infrastructure company, not an AI agency. The AI is the how. The revenue is the what. Nobody cares about the how. They care about the what.

**2. Get numbers from his two existing clients — yesterday.**
Even anonymised, even rough — "Client A recovered £X in 30 days from dead leads" or "Client B went from 4-hour average lead response time to 47 seconds." Without proof, the guarantee does all the heavy lifting. With proof, the offer becomes undeniable.

**3. Lead with the Revenue Leak Audit as a free diagnostic.**
This is the Trojan horse. The audit itself is so valuable that it builds trust and creates urgency. The prospect sees their own bleeding and the sale makes itself. This is the top-of-funnel offer.

**4. Raise prices faster than he thinks.**
£3,000 install + £500/month is starter pricing. By client 5 it should be £5,000 + £1,000/month. By client 10 it should be £7,500 + £1,500/month. The value equation supports it. The managed service model justifies it. Do not be the cheapest AI guy. Be the most valuable infrastructure partner.

**5. Create a "Revenue Recovered" metric and put it everywhere.**
Every client should have a running counter: "Total Revenue Recovered by On Auto Systems." This becomes the proof, the content, the case study, and the sales tool all in one.

**6. Build the AIOS tier as a separate brand-within-a-brand.**
AIOS is not just "more agents." It is a different product for a different buyer. On Auto is for businesses doing £500K-£5M who need their sales pipeline fixed. AIOS is for businesses doing £5M+ who want an AI intelligence layer across operations. Different positioning, different pricing, different sales process. Do not bundle them.

**7. The content strategy is simple: show the revenue leak.**
Every piece of content — LinkedIn, email, video — should show the prospect a problem they didn't know they had. "Here is how much revenue you are losing to [specific problem]." Run the numbers. Be specific. Make it hurt. Then offer the audit as the solution.

**8. Build a referral engine from day one.**
Two clients is not a customer base. It is a starting line. Offer existing clients a month free for every referral that converts. In a managed service model, the LTV is high enough that giving away one month is nothing compared to acquiring a new client.

**9. Warning: Do not scale delivery before systemising delivery.**
Callum needs to build his own internal systems before he takes on client 5. SOPs. Playbooks. Checklists. Templates. If he is personally doing every install, he will burn out at 8 clients. The irony of selling systems while not having systems is a death sentence.

**10. The grand slam offer window is NOW.**
The AI agency space is crowded but the "installed and managed revenue infrastructure" space has maybe 3 serious players globally. This positioning window will close in 12-18 months as the market matures. Callum has a head start. He needs to move fast, build proof, and own this category before someone with more resources figures out the same positioning.
