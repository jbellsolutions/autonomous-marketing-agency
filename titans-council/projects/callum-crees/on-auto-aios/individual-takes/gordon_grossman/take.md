## Positioning Analysis

Before I write a single word of copy, I need to know who we are mailing to. The list is the campaign. Everything else is secondary.

Here is what the numbers tell me about this project:

**The Market Reality (List-Level Thinking)**

The "AI automation agency" space is a bloated, undifferentiated list. Thousands of operators are mailing the same tired message — "automate your business with AI" — to the same overexposed segments. The response rates on that positioning are collapsing. When everybody says the same thing to the same list, nobody wins.

Callum's problem is not a copy problem. It is a list problem dressed up as a positioning problem.

**The Right Segment**

The ideal prospect for On Auto is NOT the business owner browsing for AI solutions. That buyer is shopping, comparing, and has been pitched by fifty agencies this month. Response rates to that segment: declining.

The profitable segment is the business owner who:
- Spends real money on lead generation (minimum £2,000/month in ad spend)
- Has a CRM with data in it (even if it is a mess)
- Has missed revenue they can FEEL — leads that went cold, follow-ups that never happened, a database of names collecting dust
- Employs at least 2-5 salespeople who are busy but not systematically supported

This is a £500K-£5M revenue business with operational friction in the sales process. They are not looking for AI. They are looking for more revenue from the leads they already pay for.

**Positioning Statement**

On Auto is not an AI agency. On Auto is installed sales infrastructure — the permanent operations layer between marketing spend and closed revenue. It does not sell artificial intelligence. It sells recovered revenue and systematic pipeline coverage.

Position against the COST OF INACTION, not the promise of technology. Every day without this infrastructure, leads are dying, follow-ups are missed, and past customers are forgotten. That is not a future problem. That is money leaving the building right now.

## Unique Mechanism

**The Mechanism: The Revenue Recovery Engine**

Every business with an active sales pipeline is running a leaking bucket. Leads come in. Some get worked. Most do not — not properly, not fast enough, not persistently enough. The leads that fall through the cracks are not lost opportunities. They are paid-for assets that depreciate to zero.

On Auto installs a **Revenue Recovery Engine** — a three-layer AI infrastructure that captures, works, and reactivates every lead that enters the business. It is not a tool. It is not a chatbot. It is a permanent operational layer that runs 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, doing the systematic work that sales teams cannot do at scale.

**Layer 1: Speed-to-Lead Capture** — Every inbound lead is engaged within 60 seconds, qualified, and routed. The response window that kills most deals (the first 5 minutes) is eliminated entirely.

**Layer 2: Systematic Follow-Up** — Every prospect in the pipeline receives persistent, multi-touch follow-up triggered by actual CRM behaviour. No lead is ever orphaned. No follow-up is ever forgotten.

**Layer 3: Dead Lead Reactivation** — The database of past leads, lost deals, and cold contacts is systematically mined. Revenue already spent on acquisition is recovered.

The mechanism works because it addresses the three places where sales revenue dies: the initial response gap, the follow-up dropout, and the dead database. These are not sales problems. They are systems problems. And systems problems require systems solutions — installed, managed, and optimised on an ongoing basis.

**Why This Mechanism Is Different**

AI agencies sell projects. On Auto installs infrastructure. The distinction is critical.

A project has a start and an end. Infrastructure is permanent. A project delivers a tool. Infrastructure delivers a result — month after month, compounding over time.

The Revenue Recovery Engine does not require the business owner to learn anything, manage anything, or change how their team sells. It runs underneath the existing sales operation, catching what falls through the cracks, working what nobody has time to work, and reactivating what has been left for dead.

## Strategic Notes

### 1. List Strategy Is Everything — Start With Warm Outbound

Callum has two clients, both from his network. The immediate priority is NOT cold outreach to strangers. It is systematic, segmented warm outreach to his extended network and second-degree connections.

**Phase 1 — The Network Audit (Weeks 1-4)**
- Map every business owner in Callum's existing network who fits the ideal profile
- Segment by: revenue size, industry, likelihood of having a lead flow problem
- Send the direct outreach letter above to the top 50 prospects, personalised
- Target: 3-5 pipeline audits from this batch alone

**Phase 2 — Referral Engine (Weeks 5-12)**
- After each successful installation, implement a structured referral request at the 30-day mark (when results are visible)
- Offer existing clients an incentive to introduce one business owner from their network
- The best list in the world is a list of people referred by satisfied customers

**Phase 3 — Cold Outbound (Month 4+)**
- Only after case studies and proof points exist from Phase 1 and 2
- Build a targeted list of UK SMBs in high-lead-volume industries (trades, professional services, property, financial services)
- Segment by ad spend indicators (businesses actively running paid ads are the highest-value targets)

### 2. The Subscription Economics Are Strong — Emphasise Them

The £500/month ongoing retainer is the real business. The installation fee covers costs. The monthly fee is where lifetime value compounds.

At £500/month with a 12-month average retention, each client is worth £6,000 in recurring revenue plus the £3,000 installation. That is £9,000 LTV per client.

At 20 clients, that is £10,000/month in recurring revenue. At 50 clients, it is £25,000/month. The economics of this model are excellent — but only if retention is high. This means the systems must deliver visible, measurable results every single month.

**Recommendation:** Build a monthly reporting mechanism that shows each client exactly how many leads were worked, how many follow-ups were sent, how many dead leads were reactivated, and what revenue can be attributed to the infrastructure. Retention is a function of perceived value. Make the value impossible to miss.

### 3. The AIOS Upsell Is The Second Subscription Tier

Do not lead with AIOS. It is too abstract for cold prospects. Lead with the Revenue Recovery Engine (the three agents). Once a client has experienced the operational layer working, THEN introduce AIOS as the upgrade path.

This is classic subscription marketing: get them in at the base tier with clear, tangible value. Upgrade them when trust is established and results are proven.

### 4. Segment and Personalise Everything

The outreach letter above is a template. It must be personalised for every segment. A trades business owner responds to different specifics than a professional services firm. The maths should be adjusted. The examples should be adjusted. The industry context should be adjusted.

One message to one list is 1990s thinking. Segmented messages to segmented lists — that is where the response rates live.

### 5. Track Every Number

Response rates. Audit conversion rates. Installation conversion rates. Monthly retention rates. Client LTV. Revenue recovered per client. Cost per acquisition.

If you are not measuring it, you are guessing. And guessing is what your competitors do. The data will tell you which segments respond, which messages work, and where to invest your time. Let the numbers make the decisions.
