## Positioning Analysis

Here is the reality of this market right now. Every business owner with a podcast subscription has heard the phrase "automate your business with AI" at least forty times in the last six months. The AI agency space is white noise. It is static on the radio dial. And when everything sounds the same, nothing gets heard.

Callum's positioning problem is not a product problem. His product is genuinely different. His problem is a *frequency* problem. He is broadcasting on the same channel as every chatbot installer and Zapier freelancer on the planet. He needs to change the station entirely.

The position I would take: **On Auto is not an AI agency. On Auto is a sales infrastructure company.** Full stop. We never say the words "AI agency." We never lead with "automation." We lead with the outcome: your sales pipeline runs itself, leads get worked in under sixty seconds, and dead prospects come back to life. The AI is the engine under the bonnet. The listener does not care what is under the bonnet. They care that the car drives itself.

In broadcast, the businesses that win are the ones that own a category. Callum does not need to compete in the AI agency category. He needs to create the "installed sales infrastructure" category and be the only name in it.

The UK SMB market at the five-hundred-K to five-million revenue range is perfect for broadcast. These are business owners who listen to podcasts in the car, have the radio on in the office, and consume audio content throughout their working day. They are reachable, repeatedly, through audio channels.

## Unique Mechanism

**The Revenue Recovery Engine**

Every other AI company sells what they build. Callum sells what he recovers. The mechanism is not "we install AI agents." The mechanism is: we find the revenue that is already leaking out of your business and we plug every hole with a system that never sleeps, never forgets, and never takes a day off.

The Revenue Recovery Engine has three components that are easy to say, easy to remember, and easy to repeat on air:

1. **The 60-Second Responder** — Every lead gets a reply in under a minute, day or night
2. **The Follow-Through System** — No prospect ever falls through the cracks again
3. **The Resurrection Protocol** — Dead leads come back to life and start buying

These are not technology descriptions. These are outcome descriptions. And in broadcast, outcomes are what sell. Nobody buys a drill because they want a drill. They buy a drill because they want a hole. Nobody buys AI agents because they want AI agents. They buy them because they want revenue they are currently losing.

The AIOS layer sits above this as the premium tier: "Once you have recovered the revenue, the Operating System shows you where to grow next." But the entry point, the thing that gets someone to pick up the phone or visit the URL, is the Revenue Recovery Engine.

## Branding Direction

**Voice:** Confident, direct, no-nonsense. Callum should sound like the bloke who shows up, looks at your sales operation, and tells you exactly where the money is falling out. Not arrogant. Not flashy. Competent. Certain. The way a plumber talks when he finds the leak.

**Tone:** Results-first. Every piece of communication should lead with a number, a timeframe, or a concrete outcome. "Sixty seconds." "Twenty-four seven." "Leads you already paid for." These are audio-friendly phrases that stick in the ear.

**How he should show up:**
- Never say "AI agency" — say "sales infrastructure"
- Never say "we build bots" — say "we install systems that recover lost revenue"
- Never lead with technology — lead with the money the listener is losing right now
- Speak in the language of the business owner, not the language of the developer
- Short sentences. Conversational rhythm. The kind of language that sounds natural when spoken aloud at a normal pace

**Brand tagline options for broadcast use:**
- "On Auto. Your pipeline never sleeps."
- "On Auto. Revenue recovery, installed."
- "Stop losing leads. Go On Auto."

## Strategic Notes

**1. Audio Is the Right Channel for This Market.**
UK SMB owners in the half-million to five-million range are busy operators. They are in the car, on the job, listening — not scrolling. Podcast advertising, radio spots, and audio-first content on social platforms are where Callum should concentrate initial broadcast efforts. The cost per acquisition on audio is often lower than digital because there is less competition for attention in the ear than on the screen.

**2. Repetition Wins in Broadcast.**
The biggest mistake new advertisers make is running a spot twelve times and declaring it did not work. Broadcast is a frequency game. The listener needs to hear the message seven to ten times before they act. Callum should commit to a sustained schedule on two or three channels rather than spreading thin across ten. Podcast sponsorships with recurring placements are ideal for his price point and audience.

**3. The Revenue Audit Is the Right Lead Magnet.**
Do not try to sell the install from the ad. Sell the audit. The audit is free, it is non-threatening, and it gives the prospect a concrete number: "Here is how much revenue you are leaking." Once they see the number, the sale closes itself. This is the direct response model — give them a reason to raise their hand, then convert them in the follow-up.

**4. Kill the AI Agency Label Immediately.**
In every piece of broadcast copy, on every podcast appearance, in every piece of content: never say "AI agency." The moment you say it, you are competing with ten thousand teenagers on Twitter who watched a YouTube video about building chatbots. The phrase "sales infrastructure" is ownable. "AI agency" is not.

**5. The 60-Second Response Stat Is Gold.**
In broadcast, you need one number that sticks. For On Auto, that number is sixty seconds. It is dramatic. It is concrete. It is easy to remember. Build the entire broadcast identity around that number. When someone hears "sixty-second lead response" and then checks their own response time, the sale has already begun.

**6. Testimonial Spots Will Scale This.**
Once Callum has four or five clients, the most powerful broadcast format is the testimonial spot. Real business owners, in their own voice, saying: "We were losing leads every night. On Auto fixed it. Last month we recovered twelve thousand pounds from leads we thought were dead." That is the spot that scales a broadcast campaign from profitable to dominant. Start collecting audio testimonials from day one.

**7. Pricing Supports a Broadcast Model.**
At two thousand five hundred pounds initial plus five hundred per month, the lifetime value of a client easily supports a cost-per-acquisition of two hundred to four hundred pounds. That is well within range for podcast and targeted audio advertising. The maths works. The only question is commitment to frequency and consistency.

**8. The AIOS Upsell Is a Retention Play, Not an Acquisition Play.**
Do not try to sell the full AI Operating System in a sixty-second spot. It is too abstract. Sell the Revenue Recovery Engine to get clients in the door. Once they see results, introduce the AIOS as the next level. In broadcast terms: the core agents are the front-end offer, the AIOS is the back-end. Always lead with what is concrete and immediate.
