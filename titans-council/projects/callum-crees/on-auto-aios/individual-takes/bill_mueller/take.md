- Straight-talking (no jargon, no fluff)
- Builder (he builds and runs things, not just advises)
- Relentless (like his systems -- always on, always following up)
- Results-obsessed (every story ends with a number or an outcome)

**Language to use:** Install. Run. Catch. Recover. Pipeline. Revenue. Leaking. Infrastructure. System. Built.

**Language to avoid:** AI agency. Automation. Chatbot. Solution. Leverage. Disrupt. Cutting-edge. Innovative. Digital transformation.

**The brand rule:** Never lead with AI. Always lead with the problem or the outcome. AI is the mechanism, not the message.

## 8. Strategic Notes

### On Email Strategy

Callum's entire outbound and nurture strategy should be built on story-driven emails like the one above. Here is why: in the AI agency space, everyone is sending feature-based emails. "Our AI agents do X, Y, Z." Nobody is telling stories about real outcomes. Stories create curiosity. Curiosity creates opens. Opens create replies. Replies create pipeline.

I would build a 7-email nurture sequence structured like this:

1. **The 9pm Lead story** (above) -- Establishes the problem and introduces the mechanism
2. **The Graveyard story** -- Focused on dead lead reactivation and the money already spent
3. **The Two Businesses story** -- Same spend, different results, the only variable is speed to lead
4. **The Follow-Up Gap story** -- How many touches most reps make vs. how many it takes to close
5. **The "Not Software" story** -- Why tools fail and installed infrastructure succeeds
6. **The Objection Killer** -- Addresses the "we tried automation" and "AI feels impersonal" objections through narrative
7. **The Decision story** -- Urgency through the cost of waiting, the leak getting bigger every month

### On the AIOS Upsell

Do not lead with AIOS in early-stage marketing. It is too abstract for cold or warm audiences. Lead with the three core agents -- speed-to-lead, follow-up, reactivation. These are tangible, visual, easy to understand through story. Once a client is installed and seeing results from the core agents, AIOS becomes a natural ascension: "Now that your pipeline is running, do you want an intelligence layer that watches the whole business?"

### On Subject Lines

Every subject line should open a curiosity loop that cannot be closed without reading the email. Never reveal the mechanism or the answer in the subject line. Tease the outcome, the cost of the problem, or the unexpected twist. My formula: Specificity + Consequence + Unanswered Question.

Examples:
- "The 9pm lead that cost twelve thousand pounds" -- Specific, consequential, makes you ask "what happened?"
- "His CRM had 800 leads nobody ever called back" -- Specific number, implication of waste, makes you ask "then what?"
- "The most expensive word in his business was 'nothing'" -- Intriguing, paradoxical, forces the open

### On Proof and Case Studies

Callum has only two clients so far. That is fine. One well-told story is worth more than fifty logos on a landing page. The email above is a case study disguised as a story. That is the template. As Callum delivers more results, each client becomes another story in the sequence. The stories ARE the proof.

### On Callum's Personal Brand

He should be visible. He should be the narrator of these stories. Not hiding behind a brand name. Not using corporate language. His face, his name, his voice. The emails come from Callum, not from "On Auto." People buy from people, especially in the services space. The brand is Callum Crees, and On Auto is what he installs.

### One Final Note

The businesses Callum is targeting are already spending money on marketing. They are already generating leads. They are already frustrated that the leads are not converting. This is the most important insight for his messaging: he is not asking anyone to spend more. He is showing them how to collect on what they have already spent. That is the story that sells itself.
