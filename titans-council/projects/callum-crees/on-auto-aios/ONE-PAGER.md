# Callum Crees — On Auto / AIOS: Titans Council Executive Summary

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Project:** callum-crees-on-auto-aios
**Brief:** Positioning copy, unique mechanism identification, and full Todd Brown build-out for On Auto — an AI sales infrastructure service launching into a crowded AI agency market. Callum needs to differentiate and own a category.

---

## The Council's Recommendation

The council of 18 legendary copywriters reached unanimous consensus on the core strategic move: **Callum must stop calling himself an AI agency immediately and permanently.** He should reposition On Auto as a **revenue infrastructure company** that installs and runs sales systems. The AI is the wiring — the revenue is the electricity.

The winning mechanism is **The Revenue Leak Protocol** — a three-phase system (Audit, Seal, Lock) that identifies and eliminates invisible revenue leaks in sales pipelines. Every business has three leaks: the Speed Gap (leads that arrive after hours and die overnight), the Follow-Up Failure (prospects who no-show and never hear from you again), and the Dead Database (hundreds of leads sitting untouched in the CRM). On Auto finds every leak, seals it with purpose-built infrastructure, and runs the system permanently.

The positioning gives Callum a 12-to-18-month window to own the "revenue infrastructure" category before the market catches up. AIOS stays hidden as a premium upsell for existing clients only — no public-facing material for six months. The go-to-market is focused: one platform (LinkedIn), one entry point (Revenue Leak Calculator), one conversion event (Revenue Leak Workshop), one close mechanism (free Revenue Leak Audit).

## Winning Positioning

> Your sales pipeline is leaking revenue through gaps your team cannot see and cannot fix by working harder. On Auto finds every leak, seals it with installed infrastructure, and runs the system permanently — so no lead goes unworked, no follow-up gets missed, and no revenue walks out the door.

**Category:** Revenue Infrastructure (not AI agency, not automation, not consultancy)
**Tagline:** "Revenue infrastructure, installed and running."
*Originated from: Todd Brown (mechanism framing), Jay Abraham (infrastructure language), Eugene Schwartz (picture-word imagery), Perry Marshall (simplification)*

## Unique Mechanism

**The Revenue Leak Protocol** — three leaks, three seals, three phases:
1. **The Audit** — Map the pipeline and quantify leaks in pounds per month
2. **The Seal** — Install AI agents at each leak point (Speed-to-Lead, Follow-Up, Reactivation)
3. **The Lock** — Manage and optimise permanently on a monthly retainer

*Originated from: Todd Brown (mechanism architecture), Eugene Schwartz ("Revenue Leak Protocol" naming), Alex Hormozi (offer stack integration)*

## Top 5 Hooks

1. **The Enquiry Form Test** — Actually fill in the prospect's own enquiry form at 11pm. Time the response. Use real data as proof of the problem. *(Jon Buchan — repeatable prospecting system)*
2. **The 2am Lead** — "Last Tuesday at 2:14am, a lead filled out the contact form. By 8:45am, the competitor had already responded — at 2:15am." *(Eugene Schwartz/Bill Mueller)*
3. **The Uncomfortable Maths** — "340 leads over 90 days. 211 never got a response within the first hour. That is 62% of their ad spend — gone." *(Perry Marshall/Gary Bencivenga)*
4. **The Graveyard** — "Somewhere in your CRM is a database of leads you paid good money for. Nobody has touched them since. That is not a dead database. That is buried revenue." *(Brian Kurtz/Bill Mueller)*
5. **The Live Demo** — "I am going to send a lead into this system right now, on camera, and you are going to watch what happens in real time." *(Greg Renker — visual proof)*

## Key Messaging Pillars

1. **Speed Captures Revenue** — 60-second response time. 391% improvement in conversion. Your competitors respond in hours.
2. **Persistence Closes Deals** — 80% of sales need 5+ follow-ups. 44% of salespeople quit after one. On Auto never quits.
3. **Recovery Monetises Sunk Cost** — Your dead leads didn't say no. They just stopped hearing from you. That's buried revenue.
4. **Install and Run (Not Build and Leave)** — Everyone else builds and walks away. On Auto stays and manages permanently.

## Council Journey

### Round 1 — First Synthesis
All 18 copywriters converged on the same core insight: kill the "AI agency" label (18/18 unanimous). The biggest debate was mechanism naming — "Revenue Leak Protocol" (Todd Brown) vs. "Installed Sales Floor" (Schwartz) vs. "Revenue Infrastructure" (Abraham). V1 selected Revenue Leak Protocol as primary with contextual variants for different channels. The Revenue Leak Audit emerged as the unanimous front-end entry point (17/18).

### Round 2 — Refinement
The council sharpened V1 with 24 specific changes. Hormozi pushed the Grand Slam Offer Stack (perceived value of GBP 17,500+ against GBP 3,000 install). Kennedy demanded quantified cost-of-inaction in every P.S. line. Buchan's Enquiry Form Test was elevated from creative idea to primary prospecting system. Marshall cut strategic recommendations from 16 down to 6 and focused channels to LinkedIn only for 90 days. Mueller built a full story sequence across the nurture emails using the "Dave" composite narrative.

### Round 3 — Final Consensus
Five minimal adjustments from V2 to final. Schwartz repositioned the one-line statement to lead with the problem image. Hormozi elevated the "Revenue Recovered" counter to a non-negotiable day-one metric. Kennedy added specific pound figures to every P.S. Mueller strengthened the founding story hook's bridge to proof. The council unanimously agreed: ship this week. Speed of execution matters more than perfection. The positioning window is 12-18 months.
